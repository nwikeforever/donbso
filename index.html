Forward

The Diocesan  Bible study outline has continued to create positive impact in the life of our members. For the past four years this study guide dealt extensively on the importance of mission and evangelism. Members have been prepared on the need to make witnessing for Christ their first priority. However, this mandate of sometimes is hampered by the circumstances which may not be solved by human wisdom alone.
It is an attempt to offer possible solutions and to alert our parishioners and other Christians on the realities of life that this study concentrates on the theme “New Born”. Today false life abound in our society. Some people erroneously display religious arrogance. Some find it difficult to tell themselves the truth about what life demands. This life is full of challenges. Storms of life sometimes occur in the life of the believer. When this occurs, what will the child of God do? As Christians, we should not jettison out faith over board when difficulties come our ways. Therefore, we should be equipped to call on our Lord Jesus Christ always to arrest every storm that will confront us.

When things get tough we should be more fervent in prayer so that our boat will not be destroyed by the storm.
Its is my sincere prayer that as you make use of this outline, you will discover the necessary tools with which to paddle your canoe through the storms of life. Jesus Christ who calms every storms of life will always assist us to succeed in all our endeavours if we remain faithful to Him.

    
Your friend and Bishop
Rt. Rev'd Dr. Owen Nwokolo
Lord Bishop, Diocese on the Niger

January 3, 2016
Season: Christmas 2
Text: 11 Corinthians 5: 14 - 17 (Tap to open bible passage)
TOPIC: A NEW BEGINNING
AIM: TO TEACH THAT THERE IS NEED FOR SPIRITUAL RENEWAL IN THE NEW YEAR.

INTRODUCTION:
The end of an era ushers in the beginning of another. As year succeeds year, December 31st (end of a year) ushers in January 1st (Beginning of a new year). New year offers well meaning people the opportunity to start afresh. Some make new year resolutions to turn new leaves, change their patterns, adopt new life style, etc.
Again, the birth of Jesus Christ ushers in a new dispensation of life which pushes the old life away and creates the new. As we celebrate His birth and enjoy the new year, it has to be done with a change of heart.

Questions for Discussion
(Tap on any question or segment with bible reference to open the bible verse)
1. Share your new year resolutions with others and say how you plan to succeed.
2. What do you think is the best resolution one can make in a new year like this. why do you think so?
3. Read 2 Cor 5:17 again and explain what you understand by (a) Old creation (Life) (Eph 4:22-23, Col 3:5-9)
(b) New creation (Life) (Eph 4:24, Col 3:12-17)
4. Discuss the benefits of the new life in Christ.

Conclusion:
New year needs new life. New life is the life in Jesus Christ. This is a life fully surrendered to God and garment of love, peace, forgiveness, humility, patience among others.

Memory Verse: Col. 3: 8 - 10

January 10, 2016
Season: Epiphany 1
Text: John 1: 1-13 (Tap to open bible passage)
TOPIC: THE TRUE LIFE AND LIGHT
AIM: TO SHOW THAT CHRIST IS THE LIFE AND LIGHT OF THE WORLD

INTRODUCTION:

The incarnation of Christ is the manifestation of the eternal word of God through which all creation derives their life and light. He came to the depraved world but due to blindness and ignorance, the world neither recognised nor received Him. In a kind of epiphany, John presented Christ as the true life and light of man who came to dispel darkness and illuminate human life and reason. He is the origin and substance of life. All people owe their life and whatever light  (light of reason, conscience, natural, etc) they have to Him.

Questions for Discussion
(Tap on any question or segment with bible reference to open the bible verse)
1a. To whom is “The Word” in verse 1 referring to?
b. What does verses 1-3 teach about him?
2. What do you think is the reason why the world rejected the light that came? John 3:19-21
3. What is the importance of Jesus saying that Christians are “the salt of the earth’ and ‘the light of the world in Matt 5:13a and 14a?
4. Can you say that Christians today are really salt of the earth and light of the world. Give reasons.

Conclusion:
Jesus is the origin and substance of life, the only source of real light to man who enlightens the world of darkness and sin and leads man to the light of God’s righteousness. Whoever follows Him will not walk in darkness (Jn 8:12)

January 17, 2016
Season: Epiphany 2
Text: Luke 2: 8 - 20
TOPIC: THE HERALD OF THE ANGEL.
AIM: TO ENCOURAGE US TO RECEIVE THE GOOD NEWS WITH JOY AND DISSEMINATE IT WITH URGENCY

INTRODUCTION:

The birth of Jesus Christ was first declared by God to the shepherds rather than the Kings and leaders of Israel through the Angel and not through  men. That stated the fact that something unique has happened. Accordingly, it was termed a gospel of good news and great joy to the world.
It also attracted Angelic band of heavenly choir who ascribed praises of glory to God and peace to the world. The shepherds were then prompted to action of searching for the baby until they found him and they made known what they have heard and seen to the people of their day.

Questions for Discussion

1. Does God still communicate with people through Angels today? Give your reasons.
2. Mention other four authentic ways through which God passes information to the people in our own time (Heb 1:22, 2 Tim 3:16-17, 1 sam 3:4,6,8,10; Gen 28:10f).
3. Why is the birth of Jesus Christ a good news of great joy to the world? (Isa 9:2-5; Lk 2:11).
b. Explain the reality of “peace to men” as announced by the Angels. (Isa 9:6-7)
4. The good news should be received without doubt and shared without delay. Discuss.

Conclusion:
If Angels should proclaim Jesus and shepherds received it with joy, and tell others, Christians of our own time should always share the good news received with others without delay

January 24, 2016
Season: Septuagesima
Text: Luke 2: 41 - 46
TOPIC: LOOKING FOR JESUS
AIM: TO ENCOURAGE CHRISTIANS TO ALWAYS LOOK/SEEK JESUS AND GO WITH HIM.

INTRODUCTION:

In line with their regular practice as devout Jews, the parents of Jesus (Joseph and Mary) went to the feast of Passover with Jesus when He was 12 years. At the end of the feast, supposing Him to be in the returning crowd, they went without Jesus. After a day’s journey, they realised that Jesus was not with them and went back looking for Him until they found Him on the third day and went with Him.

Questions for Discussion

1. How can you know one who has lost or has no Jesus in his/her life?
2. State and explain ways one can rediscover or discover Jesus in his life
3. The parents of Jesus lost Him for three days before they found Him. What special lesson has this for the children of God (Heb 6:4-8).

Conclusion:
Some people today move about without Jesus, others who have Him before have lost him without knowing. To go without Jesus is the most dangerous situation one can be in life. Check yourself, Are you without Jesus? Look for Him now and always journey with Him.

January 31, 2016
Season: Sexagesima
Text: John 2: 1 - 11
TOPIC: THE PRESENCE THAT MAKES THE DIFFERENCE
AIM: TO SHOW THAT WITH JESUS, EVERY SITUATION IS UNDER CONTROL.

INTRODUCTION:

Jesus was invited to a marriage feast at Cana in Galilee. During the occasion there arose a challenging, distressing and embarrassing situation when the wine finished. The people approached Jesus with the leading influence of his mother (Mary) who instructed them to do whatever He told them to do. Then with strict obedience to Jesus’ instructions, water was turned into wine and the situation brought under control. By doing that, Jesus demonstrated the powerful hand of God at work in his ministry.

Questions for Discussion

1a. What attitude of servants favoured the miracle (Jn 2:5-8) and
b. Explain how its contrary has been the bane of miracle, prosperity, unity and spirituality in our Churches today.
2. What unique difference did Jesus make to the man who invited Him in the marriage feast.
3. State the difference you think inviting Jesus will make in our a) marriage,( b) family (c) business (d) studies etc)
4. With biblical references, show three other instances the presence of Jesus made differences during his earthly ministry.

Conclusion:
The presence of Jesus makes a difference in everything, everywhere and at all times. He is the master of every situation, every circumstance and every challenges. Invite Him and you can be sure.

TOPIC: John 1: 29 - 34
Topic: THE BEGOTTEN AND ANOINTED
AIM: To confirm our faith that Jesus is the eternal son of God, the true Messiah and Saviour of the world.

INTRODUCTION:

From eternity, Jesus was the only begotten Son of God who was sent into the world to save the world. At his baptism, He was anointed by the Holy Spirit and revealed as the beloved son of God in whom God has good pleasure. 4. Discuss the role of Jesus Christ as the anointed son of               God. (Isa 61:1-31, Lk 4:18-19)

Conclusion:
Jesus is the message of hope for the humble, comfort for the broken hearted, freedom for those in bondage and deliverance and salvation to the world. Those who believe in Him will never perish but have eternal life.

TOPIC: Matthew 4: 1 - 11
Topic: PREPARING TO FACE TEMPTATION
AIM: TO ENCOURAGE CHRISTIANS TO ALWAYS BE READY FOR TEMPTATION

INTRODUCTION:

Temptation is an inevitable factor of human life. No matter how conscious and careful one may be, it must come as a reality of life. It comes to different people in diverse ways. it comes to both the rich and the poor, big and small, male and female. However, Christians are the hardest hit if Jesus our great master was tempted. Therefore, Christians must be ready to face it anytime it comes.

Questions for Discussion

1. What do you understand by the word “temptation”?
2. According to Matthew 4:1, Jesus was led up by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted by devil, why? (Heb 2:17-18).
3a. Who are the three chief agents of temptation to man? (Job  2:7; Mtt 4:3; Jms 1:13-15; Rom 14:13; 1 Cor 8:9)
b. Explain 5 ways which temptation can come to Christians
4a. What in your mind is the reason why Christians are mostly tempted? (2 Tim 3:12)  
b. In what practical ways can Christians prepare themselves to face temptation? (Mtt 4:2, 26:41)

Conclusion:
Temptation is a reality of life. To lose sight of this is to undue oneself. Christians should always be at alert to face it any time it comes.

Season: Lent 2
Text: Hebrews 4: 14 - 16
TOPIC: TEMPTED, BUT DID NOT SIN
AIM: TO TEACH THAT IT IS POSSIBLE FOR A CHRISTIAN TO OVERCOME/RESIST TEMPTATIONS

INTRODUCTION:

The temptation of Jesus was a lesson of the incarnation. In the incarnation, Jesus emptied himself of the form of God and took upon himself the form of man just to identify with those he came to deliver. He became man to the point of being tempted as man in hard, harsh and agonizing ways. Jesus experience real torent, torment and turture as we feel when we are severally tempted; but in all, He did not sin.  

Questions for Discussion

1a. What is the central instrument of victory in the three temptations of Jesus in Matt 4:4,7,10?
b. State how it can help those who are being tempted today.
2. What other things do you think a Christian needs to help him/her resist temptations? (Liv. 9:2, Eph. 6:10-20, James 4:7)
3. What value and benefit is the victory of Jesus over temptations to us believers? (Heb 2:17-18, 4:15).
4. Temptation to sin is not sin but yielding to temptation. Discuss.

Conclusion:
Christians have the power to overcome temptations if only we can be strong in the Lord; always believing Him, trusting Him and following Him, for He who is in us (the Holy Spirit) is greater that he (Satan)  who is in the world.

Season: Lent 3
Text: James 1: 1 - 4
TOPIC: GROWING THROUGH TEMPTATION
AIM: TO LET CHRISTIANS KNOW THAT TEMPTATIONS CAN BE OPPORTUNITIES FOR DEVELOPMENT

INTRODUCTION:

Humanly speaking, temptations do not give joy to the tempted. It is therefore viewed with feelings of self-pity,, sadness, dejection, depression and pain. However, temptations have some values especially when they come for reasons of godly life in Christ. In that case, they are occasions for growth, development and maturity.

Questions for Discussion

1. Discuss the general attitude of people to temptation and state why it is so.
2. Why did James advise Christians to rejoice at temptations and trials? (Matt 5:11-12; James 1:3-4, 12; Rev 2:10)
3. With practical life experience showed how temptation can be of value to the tempted.
4. State, with scriptural references, three instances where temptations led to promotion, growth or positive      development.

Conclusion:
Christians should uphold the great truth in the words of William Barclay that; “temptation is not meant to make us sin but to enable us to conquer sin; not to make us bad, but to make us good; not to weaken us, but to make us emerge stronger, finer and purer from the ordeal. It is never the penalty but the glory of being a man.”

TOPIC: JOY AFTER SORROW
AIM: TO ENCOURAGE CHRISTIANS THAT GOD WILL ALWAYS SEE US THROUGH IN EVERY CHALLENGE WE FACE.

Longman Dictionary of Contemporary English 5th edition defines sorrow as a feeling of great sadness, usually because someone has died or because something terrible has happened to you.
From this definition we could see that sorrow is a product of events. Our Bible passage presented the story of two men who lived in the same period but with different experiences. One had happiness first and ended in sorrow while the later had sorrow then happiness. God never promised us that we would not have difficult moments John 16:33.

    Childlessness (1 Sam 1)
    Sickness (Mark 5:21-43 Etc etc)

We are expected as Christians to pray, (1 Sam 1 :10-13, James 5: 14-16) What a friend we have in Jesus...., Paul and Silas (Acts 16:16)
3. What has God promised? Psalm 30:5, Daniel 3:28-30
4. What is the greatest joy? (Luke 15:10, Marks: 36)

Conclusion:
God has promised us joy after sorrow, all we need to do is to trust and obey Him in all our doings as He is the only one that can turn our sorrow into joy. Our song says when we walk with the lord.....

TOPIC: MINISTERING TO THE LESS PRIVILEGED
AIM: TO TEACH BELIEVERS IN CHRIST TO UPHOLD A GOOD HOSPITALITY TO THE NEEDY.

The welfare ministry is one that Christ himself emphasized. Those who carry out this work are directly affecting Him. Every good Christian will like to have the testimony like Mary who anointed the body of Christ. That you fed Jesus; clothed Him; visited Him. This will finally bring you to the place of the blessed of the father on the last day. Why not start with the needy in your neighbour hood.

April 3, 2016
Season: Easter 1
Text: 1 Peter 2: 1 - 10
TOPIC: THE CHURCH, THE CALLED PEOPLE
AIM: TO ENCOURAGE US TO GET BEYOUND THINKING OF THE CHURCH IN HUMAN TERM

INTRODUCTION:

The word "Church" is translated from the Greek word "ekklesia" which means, called out" and refers to an assembly or gathering of people that has been called together: The Church refers to Christians who have been called out of darkness into His light that we maybe obedient to His will, and follow the example of Christ. The Lord spoke to His people through the apostle Peter and used several terms in order to help them appreciate their spiritual position in Christ. He referred to them as a "chosen generation, holy nation, and people for God's own 'possession." Christians are referred to as "the people of God" and as having received God's mercy. This is the result of God's grace and love not according to our own merit.

Questions for Discussion

1. What do you understand by the term "Church?”
2. From these passages, outline other terms used to describe the church in the New Testament (Eph. 1:21-23, 1 Cor. 3:16-17, John 19:36).
3a. What are the main purposes of the Church? (1 Peter 2:9, Eph. 4:12, 1 Cor, 12:12-13].
b. What are the necessary things required of the Church as the called out people? (Matt. 28:18-20, 2Tim. 4:2, Acts 2:42-44, James 5:14-15]
4. Why should a Christian be involved in Church? (Psalm 27:4, Hebrews 10:25, Ephesians 2: 19-20]
5. To drive the message home; how did Jesus care for the church? (Ephesians 5:23-27)

Conclusion:
The Church is a gathering of certain people, namely, those that fit the description in our text (1 Pet 2:9, 10). God called us out to live as His children, and render unto Him a loving, loyal service, bearing His gentle yoke with cheerfulness and meekness, and so recommend the service of God by our conduct before men, that they shall be drawn to God by our example. God has issued the call. Have you answered to become one of those "called out?”

Season: Easter 2
Text: Deuteronomy 6: 4 - 12
TOPIC: LOVE THE LORD YOUR GOD
AIM: TO TEACH US TO LOVE GOD WITH ALL OUR HEART, SOUL AND MIGHT.

INTRODUCTION:

This passage is the meeting point of the law and the Gospel. With the exception of the word "life," love is the most important abstract term in the Bible.
